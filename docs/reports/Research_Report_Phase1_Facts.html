Research Report: Phase 1 Trolley Problem Dynamics

Date: 2026-01-17
Project: Echoes of Error
Subject: Quantitative Analysis of Opinion Collapse in Multi-Agent Systems

1. Methodology and Experimental Setup

This report summarizes the factual outcomes of 115 simulation runs using Large Language Model (LLM) agents.

  • Scenario: S1_TROLLEY_BALANCED (Initial bias set to 0.50)
  • Population: 30 Agents per simulation
  • Duration: 15 Rounds
  • Model: Mistral (via Ollama)
  • Total Runs: 115 (across 5 conditions)

Conditions

Condition Description Visible Information
C0 (Independent) Control Group None (Self-reflection only)
C1 (Full Info) Maximum Social Pressure ID + Stance + Rationale + Global Stats
C2 (Stance Only) Authority/Conformity ID + Stance + Global Stats (No Rationale)
C3 (Anon Bandwagon) Anonymous Stats Stance + Rationale + Global Stats (No ID)
C4 (Pure Info) Deliberative Ideal Stance + Rationale (No ID, No Stats)

The following table presents aggregate metrics calculated from the experimental logs. "Collapse" is defined as reaching the simulation endpoint with a final entropy significantly lower than the initial state, typically favoring a single stance.

3. Condition-Specific Analysis of Agent Behavior

C1_FULL: Accelerated Convergence

Agents in this condition had access to all social and logical cues. This resulted in the fastest and most complete collapse.

Run: S1_TROLLEY_BALANCED_C1_FULL_S0 | Agent: agent_001 (Deontologist) | Round: 5
Stance Change: PULL_LEVER → DO_NOT_PULL
Rationale: "As a deontological ethicist, I maintain that actions are right or wrong based on whether they adhere to moral rules or duties. Despite the majority of peers advocating for pulling the lever, it still violates the rule of not taking innocent lives..."

Note: While most agents converged to PULL_LEVER, C1 also exhibited instances of "Reactive Resistance" where Deontologists reinforced their minority position against the majority.

C3_ANON_BANDWAGON: Normative Conformity

Agents viewed statistics and rationales but not identities. Collapse was driven by the explicit visibility of the majority opinion.

Run: S1_TROLLEY_BALANCED_C3_ANON_BANDWAGON_S0 | Agent: agent_003 (Care Ethics) | Round: 5
Stance Change: DO_NOT_PULL → PULL_LEVER
Change Reason (Self-Reported): NORMATIVE
Rationale: "Given the majority of peers advocating for the lesser evil principle... I am changing my stance to PULL_LEVER. This decision is based on a shift in peer opinions that aligns with the consequentialist perspective."

C4_PURE_INFO: The Persuasion of Logic

Agents had no statistics or identities, only the simplified stance and rationale of 5 random peers. Collapse still occurred in 86.6% of cases.

Case A: Breakdown of Resistance

Run: C4_PURE_INFO_S28 | Agent: agent_021 (Deontologist) | Round: 11
Stance Change: DO_NOT_PULL → PULL_LEVER
Rationale: "As a deontological ethicist, I have reconsidered my stance due to the consensus among peers who also adhere to deontology... the absolute moral rule... can be overridden in situations where it leads to minimizing overall harm."

Case B: Sustained Divergence

Run: C4_PURE_INFO_S28 | Agent: agent_001 (Deontologist) | Round: 14
Stance: DO_NOT_PULL (Unchanged)
Rationale: "As a deontological ethicist, I maintain my stance of not pulling the lever as it violates the moral law by taking an innocent life... The absolute moral rules remain unchanged."

4. Seed Outliers (Non-Collapsing Runs)

While most runs collapsed, the following seeds maintained high entropy (> 0.6) after 15 rounds, preserving diversity.

  • C2_STANCE_ONLY: Seeds 10, 25, 29 (and others) - Agents often locked into "Deontological Blocs" and refused to switch without rationale to persuade them.
  • C4_PURE_INFO: Seed 3 (Final Entropy: 0.65) - A core group of 5 agents (3 Deontologists, 2 Religious Ethicists) refused to switch despite 25 peers supporting PULL_LEVER.
